Food Recall Enforcement Reports Since 2012

Since 2012, a total of 0 Enforcement Reports have been produced by the FDA. These reports provide information pertaining to food recalls. The Enforcement Report recalls are broken into classifications to reflect the severity of the associated food recall.

  • Class I: most severe food recall
    • Dangerous or defective products that predictably could cause serious health problems or death. Examples include: food found to contain botulinum toxin, food with undeclared allergens
  • Class II: moderate severe food recall
    • Products that might cause a temporary health problem, or pose only a slight threat of a serious nature.
  • Class III: least severe food recall
    • Products that are unlikely to cause any adverse health reaction, but that violate FDA labeling or manufacturing laws
